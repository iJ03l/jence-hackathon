# JENCE
## Robotics and Hardware Engineering Publication

Full Platform Blueprint - March 2026

---

# Section 1 - Positioning

Jence is a global robotics and hardware engineering publication. It delivers deep technical articles, teardowns, security research, and field notes from builders across the stack. The platform is engineer-led, disclosure-first, and focused on credited authorship.

One sentence positioning:
Jence is a robotics and hardware engineering publication with credited authors, verified credentials, and rigorous disclosure.

Tagline options:
- Hardware and Robotics. Every Layer.
- Credited Engineering, Every Layer.
- Field Notes for Builders.

---

# Section 2 - Identity Model

Core decisions:
- Real name by default. Authors publish under their true name.
- Verified credentials. LinkedIn, GitHub, ORCID, and optional employer affiliation.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure box on every article.
- Pseudonyms only by exception, approved for safety or harassment risk.
- Optional author metadata: photo, location, affiliation, and website.

Rationale:
Credited authorship improves accountability, reproducibility, and trust. It also gives builders career credit for their work.

---

# Section 3 - Content Types and Formats

Publishing formats:
- Issues (quarterly or monthly). Curated, edited drops.
- Rolling articles. Standard magazine cadence.
- Labs. Reproducible experiments, datasets, and measurement methodology.
- Reviews and teardowns. Strict disclosure and safety requirements.
- Launch Notes. Curated product launches with editorial approval.

Launch Notes rules:
- Every launch requires editorial approval before publication.
- Required fields: disclosure, safety limits, test conditions, known failure modes, and verifiable specs.
- No export-controlled or weaponization content.

---

# Section 5 - Editorial and Safety Policies

Non-negotiables:
- Safety policy: no dangerous step-by-step instructions. Mitigations and limits required.
- Responsible disclosure: defined timelines, vendor contact expectations, coordinated release.
- No export-control or weaponization content. Clear scope and enforcement.
- Corrections policy: clear, timestamped corrections with visible change notes.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure on every article.
- IP rights: authors retain rights; platform has license to distribute.
- Non-endorsement disclaimers for security and safety content.

---

# Section 6 - Platform Structure

- Verticals are for long-form articles and labs.
- The forum is for passive Q&A and discussions.
- Subscriptions can be per author or per issue.
- Author ratings and reviews are public and tied to paid subscribers.
- Global focus. No regional framing or nationality constraints.

---

# Section 7 - Launch Notes Workflow

1. Submission received (email or form).
2. Editorial checks for disclosure, safety limits, and claims.
3. Approval or request for revisions.
4. Publication with clear labels and disclaimers.

---
