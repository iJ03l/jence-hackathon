# Section 1 — The Repositioning: What Changed and Why

> The business model has not changed. The core has not changed. What changed is the language, legal structure, and positioning — because those three things determine whether Jence operates for 10 years or gets shut down in 18 months.

## The Core That Stays Intact

Jence is still a platform where people with knowledge in specific sectors share that knowledge, and other people pay to access it. Creators earn 80%. The platform earns 20%. Everything is anonymous. Everything is crypto-settled. Every sector of Nigerian society has a vertical.

That does not change. What changes is how that activity is framed, governed, and defended — because framing is not cosmetic. In law and in regulation, the difference between "anonymous insider intelligence marketplace" and "anonymous expert analysis subscription platform" can be the difference between operating legally and being investigated.

## What Moved and Why

| Element | Old Framing (v1) | Jence Framing (v2) |
|---|---|---|
| Platform Identity | Intelligence trading marketplace | Expert analysis subscription platform |
| Creator Role | Insider, source, leaker | Independent analyst, researcher, commentator |
| Content Type | Non-public intelligence, advance knowledge | Research, sector analysis, professional opinion |
| Framing of Timing | Before public announcement | Trend forecasting, directional analysis |
| Financial Content | Signals, tips, guaranteed intel | Commentary, education, not financial advice |
| Political Content | Contract intelligence, policy leaks | Government sector analysis, commentary |
| Creator Identity | Fully anonymous, no KYC | Private KYC for creators, public anonymity maintained |
| Platform Disclaimer | Platform does not verify claims | Strong disclaimers + creator accountability clause |

> **Critical fix on "Platform does not verify claims":** That clause creates a complete liability vacuum. The fix: creators self-certify compliance at onboarding, and the platform implements active moderation with a documented strike system. This shifts legal responsibility to where it belongs — the creator — while showing regulators the platform is not passive.

# Section 2 — Jence: Platform Identity & Positioning

## What Jence Is

Jence is a subscription-based platform where independent analysts, researchers, and sector specialists share expert commentary, research, and professional opinion — anonymously. Subscribers pay monthly to access creator-published content organized by sector.

The platform spans every major layer of Nigerian economic and professional life: banking and finance, government and policy, sports and recreation, digital assets, real estate, professional careers, open markets, the creator economy, agriculture, and energy.

## What Jence Is and Is Not

**Jence IS:**
- ✓ A paid research and insights platform
- ✓ A knowledge-sharing subscription hub
- ✓ A sector-based expert commentary network
- ✓ A professional analysis ecosystem
- ✓ A creator monetization infrastructure
- ✓ Anonymous — for subscribers and publicly for creators

**Jence IS NOT:**
- ✗ A whistleblowing or leak platform
- ✗ An insider trading infrastructure
- ✗ A signal or pump group
- ✗ A classified document exchange
- ✗ A financial advice service
- ✗ A money transmission or exchange service

## The One-Sentence Positioning

> **Jence is where independent Nigerian analysts share expert sector research anonymously — and get paid for it.**

## The Real Value Exchange (Core Unchanged)

The economic reality of Jence has not changed. Nigerian society has a severe information asymmetry problem. People with deep sector knowledge — whether in banking, politics, open markets, oil, or agriculture — hold insights that others with money will pay to access.

The legal repositioning does not dilute this value. An experienced banking professional sharing anonymously framed as "expert financial sector commentary" is still enormously valuable to a business owner trying to navigate CBN policy. A market trader writing anonymously about supply chain patterns is still indispensable to a retailer managing inventory. The insight does not lose value because we call it analysis rather than intelligence.

This vertical carries the lowest legal risk of all ten. Expert commentary on hiring trends, salary benchmarking, industry dynamics, career navigation, and professional development is fully protected speech in every jurisdiction. No repositioning required beyond standard disclaimers.

## 7. Open Market & Trade Sector

Commentary on supply chain patterns, import logistics observations, product availability trends, and wholesale market dynamics is commercial speech — publishable, valuable, and legally clean.

## 8. Creator Economy & Digital Business Sector

Lowest risk vertical alongside career. Platform strategy, monetization commentary, content trend analysis, and brand deal market observations are entirely permissible commercial speech. No significant repositioning required.

## 9. Agriculture & Food Sector

Harvest intelligence, commodity price trend analysis, and supply chain pattern observation are all legitimate market commentary. Frame all content as market observation and trend analysis, not as advance government information.

## 10. Oil, Gas & Energy Sector

This vertical requires the most careful moderation given national security implications. Content must stay at the level of market and sector analysis.

**What remains:**

- Fuel pricing trend commentary

- Generator market and alternative energy commentary

- International oil price impact commentary on the Nigerian economy

**What is removed:** Content implying access to NNPC confidential operations, pipeline security information, or sensitive infrastructure data — flagged and removed immediately.

# Section 4 — Creator Framework & Accountability Structure

## The KYC Decision: Why Creators Cannot Be Fully Anonymous

In the original blueprint, creators were fully anonymous — no real name, no identity, no accountability trail. That model maximises creator willingness to share but creates a severe platform liability problem.

If a creator publishes content that constitutes a breach of employment contract, a violation of securities law, or a defamation claim — and the platform has no way to identify that creator — the platform becomes the only liable party regulators or plaintiffs can pursue. That is an existential risk.

> **The fix:** Private KYC for creators. Public anonymity fully maintained. Creators submit verified identity to the platform only. Their public presence remains a pseudonym. Subscribers never see their real identity. But Jence knows who they are — and can cooperate with legitimate legal processes if required. This single change dramatically reduces platform liability.

## Creator KYC Process

- Creator submits Nigerian NIN, BVN, or passport — stored encrypted, never displayed publicly
- Creator receives a pseudonymous username — their only visible identity on the platform
- Creator signs the Jence Creator Agreement digitally at onboarding
- KYC data is stored separately from creator content — access requires legal compulsion or verified law enforcement request
- Creator can change their public username at any time without affecting their KYC record

## Creator Self-Certification (Required at Onboarding)

Before publishing any content, every creator must actively confirm — not through a passive checkbox, but through a signed declaration:

> *"I confirm that: (1) I am not disclosing confidential information obtained through my employment or under any non-disclosure agreement. (2) I am not violating any fiduciary duty owed to any employer, client, or institution. (3) I am not sharing classified government material of any kind. (4) I am not providing financial or investment advice without the appropriate license or regulatory authorisation. (5) I take full personal responsibility for the accuracy and legality of any content I publish on Jence. I understand that Jence will cooperate fully with legitimate legal processes requiring identification of content publishers."*

## Creator Content Standards

| PERMITTED CONTENT | PROHIBITED CONTENT |
|---|---|
| Research based on publicly observable information | Confidential employer or client information |
| Expert opinion and professional sector commentary | Classified government documents |
| Statistical analysis and data interpretation | Content implying guaranteed financial returns |
| Trend forecasting based on observable signals | Match-fixing or betting manipulation |
| Anonymous income reports with no identifying detail | Doxxing, harassment, or personal targeting |
| Sector risk analysis and due diligence frameworks | Fraud tutorials or scam facilitation of any kind |
| Public-record-based government sector research | Corruption allegations without verifiable evidence |
| Agricultural and commodity market observation | Ponzi, pyramid, or multi-level scheme promotion |

## Strike System

| Strike | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Strike 1 | Content removed. Creator notified with specific policy clause violated. 7-day content restriction. |
| Strike 2 | Content removed. 30-day publishing suspension. Revenue withheld during suspension period. |
| Strike 3 | Permanent account termination. Revenue forfeited. KYC data flagged for legal review if content warrants it. |
| Immediate Ban | Match-fixing, fraud facilitation, classified document sharing, or doxxing — immediate termination without warnings. |

# Section 5 — Minimum Viable Legal Setup

## Step 1: Company Incorporation

> Do not launch Jence as an individual. The platform handles financial transactions, user data, and content liability. You need a legal entity between you and all three.

**Option A — Nigeria First (Fastest):** Register with CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission). ₦30,000–80,000 depending on type. Takes 1–2 weeks. Sufficient for MVP launch.

**Option B — Offshore Entity (More Protective):** UK Limited Company (~£50, 24 hours online), UAE Free Zone ($3,000–8,000), or Estonia e-Residency (€100 card + registration fees). Gives better regulatory clarity on crypto, stronger investor confidence, and reduced exposure to arbitrary regulatory action at scale.

**Recommended path:** CAC registration immediately for MVP. Offshore entity by Month 4 once revenue validates the model.

## Step 2: Legal Documents (Non-Negotiable)

Do not use templates downloaded from the internet for this platform. Engage a Nigerian fintech or technology lawyer.

| Document | What It Must Cover for Jence Specifically |
|---|---|
| Terms of Service | Platform's role as neutral host only. Creator responsibility for content legality. Subscriber assumption of risk. Dispute resolution mechanism. Governing law clause. |
| Privacy Policy | KYC data collection and encrypted storage. Pseudonymous subscriber data handling. Crypto wallet address treatment. NDPA compliance. Breach notification procedure. |
| Creator Agreement | Self-certification clause. Content standards. Revenue split terms. KYC consent. Platform cooperation with legal processes. Strike system and termination conditions. |
| Subscriber Agreement | No guarantee of accuracy. Educational use only. No financial or investment advice. Crypto payment finality. Refund policy (limited). |
| Disclaimer Layer | Displayed on every creator post, every vertical landing page, and at account creation. |

**Budget:** ₦200,000–500,000 for a competent Nigerian technology lawyer to draft these properly. This is not optional — it is your first line of defence.

## Step 3: Data Protection Compliance (NDPA)

Jence collects sensitive data: creator KYC documents, subscriber pseudonymous identities, wallet addresses, and IP logs. The Nigeria Data Protection Act requires compliance regardless of whether users are fully anonymous.

- Appoint a Data Protection Officer or designate a responsible person internally
- Document exactly what data you collect, why, and how long you keep it
- Implement encryption for KYC data storage — mandatory, not optional
- Create a data breach response procedure — NDPA requires breach reporting within 72 hours of discovery
- User consent must be explicit and granular, not buried in terms
- Users must have a mechanism to request data deletion (right to erasure)

## Step 4: Crypto Payment Positioning

> **Jence's legal position:** "We are a content subscription platform that accepts cryptocurrency as a payment method, processed via third-party payment infrastructure. We do not hold user funds, operate a wallet service, or facilitate currency exchange."

**To maintain this position:**
- Use a third-party processor: Breet, NOWPayments, or Coinbase Commerce
- Do not hold subscriber funds beyond the time required to process and split payments
- Auto-distribute creator revenue on a daily or weekly cycle
- Do not market the platform using language that implies financial services or currency exchange

## Step 5: Financial Vertical Disclaimers

> *"Content published on Jence is for informational and educational purposes only. Nothing on this platform constitutes financial, investment, legal, tax, or political advice. Jence does not verify the accuracy of creator content. Past analysis does not guarantee future outcomes. Users assume full responsibility for any decisions made based on content accessed through Jence. Always consult a licensed professional before making financial decisions."*

This disclaimer appears on every creator post, every vertical landing page, and at account creation.

## Step 6: Political Content Governance

The political vertical requires its own additional governance layer. Passive moderation here is not acceptable.

- All political vertical posts are reviewed within 24 hours of publication
- No unverified specific corruption allegations against named individuals
- No doxxing — publishing personal details of any private individual
- No content that could constitute incitement or targeted harassment
- No classified government material — immediate removal and KYC review if found
- Posts making specific factual claims must identify the basis of the claim

## Step 7: Sports & Gambling Risk Mitigation

| PROHIBITED IN SPORTS VERTICAL | PERMITTED IN SPORTS VERTICAL |
|---|---|
| Match-fixing coordination of any kind | Statistical analysis using publicly available data |
| "Insider" referee or official information | Public data-based probability modeling |
| Guaranteed win claims | Opinion-based forecasting with risk disclosure |
| Coordinated betting pool facilitation | Bookmaker odds comparison and market commentary |
| Unlicensed tipster services presented as certainties | Form analysis, public injury reports, observable team dynamics |

## Step 8: Offshore Jurisdiction (Recommended at Scale)

If Jence reaches meaningful revenue — ₦10M+ monthly — consider an offshore holding structure. This is about regulatory clarity and investor comfort, not tax evasion.

| Jurisdiction | Why It Works for Jence | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|---|
| UK Limited Company | Strong contract law, crypto clarity improving, credible for investors | £50–300 to register, £13/year filing |
| UAE Free Zone | Crypto-friendly, zero corporate tax, strong protections | $3,000–8,000 depending on zone |
| Estonia e-Residency | EU-based, digital-first infrastructure, strong data laws | €100 e-Residency card + company registration fees |

# Section 7 — Compliance-First MVP Structure

## Must Have Before Launch (Non-Negotiable)

- CAC registration or equivalent — do not collect money as an individual
- Terms of Service and Privacy Policy — reviewed by a lawyer, not a template
- Creator Agreement with self-certification clause
- Creator KYC system — encrypted storage, separated from content
- Financial disclaimer on every post, every vertical, every creator page
- Moderation system — even if manual at MVP stage, it must be documented
- Third-party crypto payment processor — do not build your own at launch
- Strike system — documented and enforceable before first creator goes live

## Can Be Built Post-Launch (Important But Not Blocking)

- Offshore holding structure — implement by Month 4 if revenue validates
- Automated content moderation tools — manual is acceptable at small scale
- Full data breach response procedure — document within first 30 days post-launch
- NDPA formal Data Protection Officer designation — within first 60 days
- Creator analytics dashboard — valuable but not legally required at launch
- Formal AML policy — required before significant transaction volumes

## The Compliance-First Launch Homepage Positioning

> **Suggested headline:** *"Jence — Expert sector research, published anonymously. Paid content from independent analysts across banking, markets, policy, and more. Subscribe to the insights Nigeria's professionals keep to themselves."*

**What this copy does legally:** It identifies the content as "research" (protected speech), identifies the creators as "independent analysts" (not insiders or leakers), frames the value as insight rather than advance information, and implies subscriber value without making guarantees.
